Teaching activities

As part of my Ph.D. training, I participate in diverse teaching activities. In fact, I am passionate about teaching and learning, and I am extremely interested by the possible contributions of new technologies in university-level pedagogical techniques, e.g. flipped classroom, clicker sessions, etc.

Teaching assistance, EQ2300 Digital Signal Processing

Up until now, the main bulk of my work in teaching activities has been the teaching assistance of the subject EQ2300 Digital Signal Processing in the Fall semesters from 2014 to 2018. As part of this task, I have guided students through approximately 100 hours of exercise sessions and 20 hours of laboratory sessions, and I have even lectured for 4 hours during the fall semester of 2017 (accumulating an approximate number of 140 hours of class preparation). Furthermore, I have taken an active role in the development of new course material, and spent up to 45 h developing a new exercise collection, and thus, a new set of exercise sessions, that brought together the content in the lectures and the exercise assignments. Finally, I have dedicated roughly 20 hours to private tutoring and 100 hours to the grading of projects and exams.

Supervision of student theses

Additionally, I supervise M.Sc. and B.Sc. theses in practical machine learning and optimization topics.
